East Africa School of Astronomy (EA-SA)



In the past, there had been a number of trainings and workshops of astronomy conducted in the region in different format for several years with individuals as well as institutional efforts in the region . However, there was no school or training center formed so far that continuously provide this training in a well coordinated and sustainable manner. It was also difficult to monitor and measure the impact it has created in the region. It ,is therefore, needs a mechanism to create regional platform and regional node of coordination, to develop long-term collaborations at regional and international level by launching regional Astronomical School.

Thus, the main idea of the project is to strengthen the east Africa region astronomical community partnership via science diplomacy and deliver short-term training that focus on improving skills and competitiveness of early career BSc and MSc graduates and students in astronomy and science related fields with well organized regional platform called East Africa School of Astronomy (EA-SA).

East Africa School of astronomy program contains training of how to use and practice Astronomical instruments; apply Astronomical softwareâ€™s, big data analysis that can be applied to multi sector fields at governmental and industry level that fits to the modern technology and global market demand. It also incorporates science communication skills to enhance the training interpersonal relation as well as science communications skills.

The school will be organized and coordinated by East Africa regional office of Astronomy for Development (EA-ROAD) in partnership with East Africa Astronomical Society (EAAS). The school is planned to be conducted biennially and it is linked with the general Assembly (GA) of East Africa Astronomical Society (EAAS). It is planned to be conducted for one week prior to the EAAS general Assembly. The target audience of the project is early carrier BSC graduates and MSc students from Astronomy and Science background. It is expected that at least 25 students will participated in the School every year. The School Course will be delivered by partnering organizations from South Africa Astronomical Observatory (SAAO), the Square Kilometer Array (SKA), Ethiopian Space Science and Technology Institute (ESSTI) and others.

The East Africa Astronomy School is open for all participants from the region. The school will consider equal participation of women and people with disability. It will consider half of the trainee to be female and hence it promotes empowerment of women and inclusiveness. Priority should also be given for participants who are underprivileged and coming from a country where astronomy is not well established.

The East Africa School of Astronomy (EASA) will act as platform that used to engage Astronomical Institutions, Professional Society, science educators and advocators, governmental and nongovernmental agencies to support the project and realize the EASA training in continuous manner.

Intended Outcome of The School


  • Expose trainer about the new areas of research and current technologies in the field of Astronomy , instrumentation and big data analysis and computing
  • Improves skill how to utilize multi-wavelength Astronomical instrumentations, software, and big data science
  • Increase the trained workforce of the region in modern technology sector that compete to global market
  • Enhance regional partnership, cooperation and effective utilization of resources
  • Improve interpersonal and science communication skills
  • Create networking and collaboration platform

EA-SA Program format


The following are the general program format where the school is designed ( it is open for comment and further enrichment)

  • Introduction to Science Diplomacy and Science Communication
  • Introduction of Multi wavelength Astronomy
  • Astronomical Instrumentation and Observing techniques
  • Data reduction and analysis Methods
  • Astronomical software package training
  • Data-intensive Science and big data analysis

Structure of EA-SA
(Governance of the school)


The school will have Advisory board, Chair and Co-Chairs and Volunteer contributors

Advisory board consists of scientists from regional and international scientific community who are willing to contribute for the success of the EA-SA.
Advisory board will have role of leadership and advise of the school in different categories such as program content design, fund raising, media and communication, Education and public outreach, soliciting volunteers for scientific contribution.

Chair (Director) is responsible to oversight the overall activities of the School and in charge of the school administration and official liaison of the school.

Co-Chairs (Co-Directors) are responsible in coordinating and directing activities under the assigned categories of school (such as program content design, fund raising, Education and public outreach, etc)

Potential Collaborators and Supporters


The following organizations and institutes assumed to be potential supporter and collaborators of the School :

  • East Africa Astronomical Society (EAAS)
  • Ethiopian Space Science and Technology Institute (ESSTI)
  • South Africa Astronomical Observatory (SAAO), South Africa
  • HartRAO, SKA, South Africa
  • East Africa Astronomical Society (EAAS) and East Africa Astronomical Research Network (EAARN), East Africa
  • Institute of Astrophysics of the Canary Islands, ESA and other Collaborators

The collaborators could support the school in terms of provision of their professional/technical experts, remotely accessing their observatories, open source and educational materials and other necessary supports where necessary .
